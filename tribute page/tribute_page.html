Yusuf al-Qaradhawi

A prominent scholar known for his contributions to Islamic thought and jurisprudence.

Biography

  • His real name is Yusuf bin Abdullah bin Ali bin Yusuf.
  • Born on September 9, 1926, in Saft Turab rural village, Nile Delta, Egypt
  • Lost his father at the age of two and was raised by his mother and uncle
  • Become an orphane at young age
  • Read and memorized the entire Quran before the age of ten
  • Studied at the Institute of Religious Studies at Tanta, graduating after nine years
  • Attended Al-Azhar University in Cairo, graduating in 1953
  • Earned a diploma in Arabic Language and Literature in 1958
  • Completed his PhD thesis in 1973 with First Merit
  • Died at September 26, 2022

Key Contributions

  • Founder of the International Union of Muslim Scholars.
  • Author of numerous books on Islamic jurisprudence and ethics.
  • Advocate for Islamic moderation and understanding.
  • Influenced contemporary Muslim thought through scholarship and media.
